Why AI Literacy Belongs in High School

Students are already encountering AI in school, at home, online, and in the tools they use every day. The question is no longer whether students will use AI. The question is whether they will learn to use it honestly, safely, and thoughtfully.

AI literacy helps students understand what AI can do, where it can mislead them, how to verify information, how to protect privacy, and how to use technology in ways that strengthen learning instead of replacing it.

AI should help students think better, not think less.

Module B

Module B: AI + Coding Starter Kit

The AI + Coding Starter Kit helps students use AI as a coding coach, not a code replacement. The resources support Python debugging, better coding questions, student ownership, verification, ethical AI use, and classroom-ready programming routines for grades 8-12.

AI should help students code better, not avoid learning code.

This module is designed for Computer Science Foundations, introductory Python, AI literacy, and digital ethics instruction. It reinforces the idea that AI can explain, hint, debug, and generate practice, but students must still read code, make decisions, test results, document changes, and own the final work.

Module C

Cybersecurity & Digital Ethics Starter Kit

Student-safe cybersecurity instruction for life skills, digital citizenship, and career-connected learning.

The Cybersecurity & Digital Ethics Starter Kit introduces students to practical, defensive, and age-appropriate cybersecurity habits. Students learn how to spot phishing attempts, protect accounts, reflect on digital footprints, explore cybersecurity careers, and discuss AI-related security risks responsibly.

Cybersecurity should be taught as a life skill, a digital citizenship skill, and a career-connected technology skill. This module keeps instruction student-safe, defensive, ethical, and connected to Tennessee Computer Science Foundations standards.

Standards Connection: These resources may support Tennessee Computer Science Foundations (C10H11) standards related to occupations, troubleshooting, social/legal/ethical issues, data security, security breaches, and security practices. Final alignment should be confirmed against local district pacing and course placement.

My Teaching Approach

My teaching approach combines real-world technology experience with practical classroom instruction. I focus on helping students build confidence, ask better questions, solve problems, and connect technology skills to future opportunities.

In AI literacy, computer science, and cybersecurity, students need more than tools. They need judgment. They need structure. They need practice. They need to understand when technology helps and when human thinking must stay in charge.
